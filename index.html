Leadership

From bedside to algorithms — a clinician’s evolution into computation

AD

Adrian Charles Demidont, DO

Founder & CEO

The Clinical Foundation

For over two decades, Dr. Demidont practiced as a board-certified infectious diseases physician, witnessing firsthand how underserved populations are left behind by inequitable systems. As Chief Medical Officer at Anchor Health Initiative and Principal Medical Scientist at Gilead Sciences, he dedicated his clinical and academic career to building equitable healthcare — pushing boundaries and disrupting systems that failed the patients who needed them most.

The Computational Turn

In 2018, a colleague introduced Dr. Demidont to the power of data science and machine learning. The realization was immediate: these tools could amplify everything he had spent decades fighting for — equity, access, and evidence-based care. What began as curiosity became a systematic transformation into a computationalist: someone who builds, validates, and deploys computational tools to solve real-world problems.

Evidence of a Computationalist

The transition from clinician to computationalist is demonstrated not by credentials alone, but by building and shipping:

Built

Noctua — a full-stack NLP application for automated Vancouver citation auditing, from PDF/DOCX parsing to two-column gutter detection, deployed in production on Railway with Stripe billing integration. All research code is open-source on GitHub.

Published

6 computational manuscripts — 1 accepted (Viruses, MDPI: CDS algorithm validation at 21.2M patients), 5 under review at Viruses, J Infectious Diseases, BMC Public Health, PLOS ONE, and PLOS Computational Biology, spanning mathematical modeling, Monte Carlo simulation, algorithmic bias, and neurometabolic signal processing. All but one available as preprints on Preprints.org and bioRxiv.

Trained

MIT Sloan Executive Education in AI for Healthcare and MITx Data Science & Machine Learning certification — formal training in the computational methods now central to his work.

Founded

Nyx Dynamics — an AI consulting company that bridges clinical expertise with computational tools, serving healthcare organizations navigating algorithm deployment and equity validation.

Why It Matters

The convergence of deep clinical experience with computational capability is rare. Most AI companies lack clinical context; most clinicians lack the ability to build and validate algorithms. Dr. Demidont operates at the intersection — a physician who writes the code, a data scientist who understands the patient.

Principles

Context Matters

An algorithm isn't good or bad per se – it is just a tool. Who is using it, and for which patients? We go beyond accuracy metrics.

The Readiness Paradox

Rigorous validation exposes uncertainty rather than eliminating it. Computationally ready ≠ clinically proven.

Ethics Cannot Be Automated

There cannot be a universal checklist – clinical context is too important. Human judgment will always be essential.

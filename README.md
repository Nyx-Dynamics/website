## Overview

This repository contains the manuscript and supplementary materials for a structured evidence synthesis proposing a paradigm shift in how the PrEP cascade is conceptualized for long-acting injectable HIV prevention (LAI-PrEP).

The paper formally defines the **bridge period** — the 2–8 week gap between LAI-PrEP prescription and first injection — as a measurable cascade step and the primary implementation failure point for LAI-PrEP. While oral PrEP fails predominantly *after* initiation due to adherence challenges, LAI-PrEP inverts this pattern: **47% of patients are lost *before* they ever receive their first injection**.

## Key Contributions

1. **Formal definition** of the bridge period as a measurable cascade step — no prior publication has named, defined, or proposed measurement criteria for this temporal gap
2. **Standardized monitoring metrics** enabling cross-site comparison: bridge period success rate, time to injection, attrition characterization, population-stratified completion rates
3. **Population-stratified attrition projections** across six populations (adolescents, women, PWID, transgender individuals, MSM, Sub-Saharan Africa) derived from convergent evidence and validated computationally in the companion paper
4. **21-intervention evidence library** organized by six mechanism categories (eliminate, compress, navigate, remove barriers, clinical support, system-level) with effect sizes, evidence tier ratings, and implementation complexity
5. **Analytical framework** situating LAI-PrEP implementation within Proctor et al.'s implementation outcomes taxonomy

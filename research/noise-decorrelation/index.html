01 — The Paradox

The Assault Without the Damage

During acute HIV infection, the brain endures a neurotoxic storm. Standard models predict neuronal loss. But it doesn't happen.

What happens during acute HIV

Massive neuroinflammation. Elevated glutamate excitotoxicity. Mitochondrial stress. Oxidative damage. Every marker says the brain should suffer — yet NAA, the gold-standard neuronal health metabolite, remains largely preserved. No quantitative model existed to explain why.

02 — The Mechanism

Noise Decorrelation as a Firebreak

We propose that shorter noise correlation during acute infection paradoxically protects neurons — like a firebreak limiting the spread of a wildfire.

The core idea

In neuronal microtubules, quantum-scale noise correlations determine how far damage signals propagate. During acute HIV, inflammation disrupts these correlations — shortening the correlation length ξ. Counterintuitively, this reduced coherence limits the coordinated spread of damage, preserving overall neuronal function.

05 — Significance

From Paradox to Prediction

This framework transforms a 35-year clinical observation into testable, falsifiable predictions — connecting quantum-scale microtubule dynamics to macroscopic neurological outcomes.

What this enables

Targeted MRS studies can now directly test the noise decorrelation hypothesis. The model generates specific quantitative predictions for what should be observed in acute vs. chronic cohorts. If confirmed, it bridges condensed matter physics and clinical neuroscience through a single measurable parameter.

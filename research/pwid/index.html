01 — The Prevention Paradox

A Drug That Works — In a System That Doesn't

Long-acting injectable PrEP (LAI-PrEP) is over 96% effective in clinical trials. Yet for people who inject drugs, the actual probability of sustained prevention is 0.003%. This 5,434-fold disparity isn't a failure of medicine — it's a failure of architecture.

The structural access crisis

Barriers like criminalization and housing instability create a median 72-hour access delay that structurally closes PEP as a prevention strategy for PWID. The biological window for parenteral exposure is 12–24 hours. By the time structural barriers are navigated, the window has already closed.

Criminalization is the lead barrier

At 38.4%, criminalization is the single largest contributor to prevention cascade collapse. It doesn't just create a legal barrier — it structurally delays access past the biological point of no return. Decriminalization alone won't solve the problem (because other barriers remain), but no solution works without it.

Policy is the mathematical prerequisite

The simulation demonstrates that structural reform is required before pharmaceutical advances can become effective for PWID. Under current policy: prevention success is 0.003%. With full harm reduction: 9.55%. With full harm reduction plus algorithmic debiasing: 18.57%. Progressive policy, not better drugs, is the only path.

04 — Contributions

What This Study Provides

  • First Monte Carlo simulation quantifying structural barriers to HIV prevention for PWID
  • Identification of criminalization (38.4%) as the dominant barrier in the 8-step cascade
  • Proof that 93.1% of prevention failures are architectural, not biological or pharmacological
  • 5-year outbreak projections: 73% national, 92.4% Pacific Northwest regional probability
  • Policy simulation showing that harm reduction alone improves success 3,183-fold (to 9.55%)
  • Mathematical demonstration that pharmaceutical advances cannot succeed without structural reform

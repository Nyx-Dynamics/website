The 47% Attrition Cliff

A visual walkthrough of how LAI-PrEP’s extraordinary 96% efficacy is undermined by a 47% bridge period attrition rate — and how a clinical decision support tool validated at UNAIDS scale (21.2M individuals) can recover 4.1 million failed transitions.

Slide 01 — Title
Computational validation of a clinical decision support algorithm for LAI-PrEP bridge period navigation at UNAIDS PrEP target scale. Published in Viruses 2026, validated on 21.2 million simulated patients — the exact UNAIDS 2025 global PrEP target.
Slide 02 — Key Numbers
Four numbers that define the scale. Validated on 21.2M patients at the exact UNAIDS target. 81.6% relative improvement in bridge period success. An estimated ~100,000 infections prevented annually. All at policy-grade statistical precision of ±0.018 pp.
Slide 03 — The Implementation Paradox
LAI-PrEP inverts the failure mode. Oral PrEP fails after initiation (adherence). LAI-PrEP fails before initiation — 47% of patients are lost during the 2–8 week bridge period between prescription and first injection. This shifts responsibility from patient behavior to health system design.
Slide 04 — Decision Support Architecture
Five-step point-of-care workflow in under 30 seconds. Patient presentation → risk stratification → barrier assessment (13 structural barriers) → intervention recommendation (library of 21 evidence-based interventions) → outcome prediction. Configuration-driven logic enables rapid adaptation to local contexts.
Slide 05 — Policy-Grade Technical Architecture
Built for policy-grade reliability. Python-based streaming engine processes 83,800 patients/second. JSON export for full auditability with open-source codebase. 100% pass rate on 18/18 edge case unit tests. Clinical parameters separated from algorithmic logic for rapid adaptation.
Slide 06 — Progressive Validation
Four-tier progressive validation demonstrates convergence. From 1K (±2.6 pp) to 1M (±0.09 pp) to 10M (±0.028 pp) to 21.2M (±0.018 pp) — a 144× precision improvement. Processing speed stable at ~80K patients/second across all tiers.
Slide 07 — Global Impact at UNAIDS Scale
From 23.96% to 43.50% — an absolute gain of +19.54 percentage points. This translates to 4.1 million additional successful transitions globally, ~100,000 HIV infections prevented annually, and an 81.6% relative improvement. The tool closes 23.4% of the global UNAIDS PrEP gap.
Slide 08 — Structural Barrier Dose-Response
Structural barriers follow a dose-response relationship. Each additional barrier reduces bridge period success by -7.74 percentage points. 85.7% of the global patient population faces ≥1 structural barrier. Below 4 barriers, patients cross the clinical threshold where success drops below 15%.
Slide 09 — The Equity Engine
The most vulnerable populations see the greatest gains. PWID: +265% relative improvement (10.4% → 37.8%). Adolescents: +147% (16.3% → 40.3%). Systematic implementation support narrows rather than widens health equity gaps at global scale.
Slide 10 — Regional Health Equity
Regions with the highest burden see the highest returns. Sub-Saharan Africa (62% of global patients) achieves +91.2% relative improvement, from 21.7% to 41.5%. Regions with the greatest need demonstrate the greatest marginal return on investment from bridge period navigation.
Slide 11 — Site-Specific Adaptation
Context-aware adaptation for local epidemics. Pacific Northwest case study with 35% methamphetamine co-use prevalence. The tool adapts to local network mixing dynamics and injection frequency patterns. Stochastic avoidance failure: network conditions are already sufficient to sustain epidemic transmission.
Slide 12 — Outbreak Forecasting & Prevention Levers
50% probability of major outbreak by 2027 without intervention. The three most influential levers: methamphetamine effect on network density, incarceration rate, and SSP coverage. Policy must target these specific structural drivers to bend the curve.
Slide 13 — The ROI of Prevention
10.5:1 five-year return on investment. The impact cascade: 4.1 million additional successful transitions → ~100,000 HIV infections prevented annually → $40 billion in lifetime treatment costs saved. Bridge period navigation is one of the highest-value interventions in the PrEP implementation landscape.
Slide 14 — Validated. Scalable. Open.
Four pillars of implementation readiness. Validated at 21.2M individuals with policy-grade precision. Equity-focused with greatest benefits for PWID (+265%) and adolescents (+147%). High impact with potential to prevent ~100K infections/year. Implementation ready with open-source code under MIT License.
Slide 15 — Next Steps
The critical next step is translating computational potential into clinical reality. Seeking prospective pilot sites for Phase 1 validation: 50–100 patients, 3–6 months. Rigorous prospective validation, continuous evidence monitoring, and equity-focused implementation.

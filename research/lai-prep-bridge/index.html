Bridging the Gap in Global LAI-PrEP Implementation

A clinical decision support algorithm validated at UNAIDS target scale (21.2 million individuals) that increases LAI-PrEP bridge period success rates by 81.6%, with potential to prevent ~100,000 HIV infections annually.

Published Article GitHub Repository Zenodo Database ← All Research

Slide 01 — Overview
A clinical decision support algorithm for LAI-PrEP bridge period navigation. Validated at the full UNAIDS global PrEP target scale of 21.2 million individuals. Published in Viruses (MDPI), 2026. Open-source at github.com/Nyx-Dynamics/lai-prep-bridge-tool-pub.
Slide 02 — Key Results
Four headline numbers define the tool's impact. 21.2M patients validated at full UNAIDS scale. 81.6% relative improvement in bridge period success (23.96% to 43.50%). ~100,000 infections prevented annually at full implementation. ±0.018 pp statistical precision at policy-grade accuracy.
Slide 03 — The Implementation Paradox
LAI-PrEP solves the adherence problem but introduces a new upstream vulnerability. Oral PrEP barriers occur after initiation (daily pill burden). LAI-PrEP barriers occur before injection (navigation & logistics). The 2–8 week bridge period loses 47.1% of patients to scheduling, insurance authorization, and HIV testing requirements.
Slide 04 — Clinical Decision Support Architecture
A five-stage pipeline from patient presentation to outcome prediction. Patient demographics and PrEP status feed into risk stratification (4 tiers), barrier assessment (13 structural barriers), intervention recommendation (21 evidence-based interventions), and outcome prediction. Processing time: <30 seconds — enabling point-of-care decision support.
Slide 05 — Policy-Grade Technical Architecture
Built for real-world deployment. Python-based streaming engine processes 83,800 patients/second. Full JSON export for auditability. 100% pass rate on 18/18 edge-case unit tests. Configuration-driven logic separates clinical parameters from algorithmic logic for rapid adaptation.
Slide 06 — Progressive Validation
Validation scaled progressively from 1K to 21.2M patients. Margin of error decreased from ±2.600 pp to ±0.018 pp — a 144× precision improvement — while processing speed remained stable at ~80,000 patients/second. Results enter the policy-grade precision zone at 10M+ scale.
Slide 07 — Global Impact at UNAIDS Scale
Bridge period success rates nearly double globally. Baseline success of 23.96% rises to 43.50% with algorithm-guided interventions — a +19.54 percentage point absolute gain. This translates to 4.1 million additional successful transitions and ~100,000 infections prevented annually.
Slide 08 — Structural Barriers
Each additional structural barrier reduces success by -7.74 pp. A clear dose-response relationship: patients with 0 barriers achieve ~45% success, while those with 5+ barriers fall below the 15% clinical threshold. 85.7% of the global patient population faces at least one structural barrier.
Slide 09 — The Equity Engine
The most marginalized populations see the greatest gains. PWID: +265% relative improvement (10.4% to 37.8%). Adolescents (16–24y): +147% (16.3% to 40.3%). Systematic implementation support narrows health equity gaps rather than widening them.
Slide 10 — Regional Health Equity
Sub-Saharan Africa — 62% of the global patient burden — sees a +91.2% relative gain. Regions with the highest HIV burden demonstrate the highest marginal return on investment from bridge period navigation. The tool preferentially benefits the populations that need it most.
Slide 11 — Site-Specific Adaptation
Configuration-driven logic enables site-specific adaptation. Pacific Northwest case study: 35% methamphetamine co-use prevalence alters network mixing dynamics and injection frequency. Network conditions already exceed the critical outbreak threshold (0.35) — stochastic avoidance failure means luck will inevitably run out.
Slide 12 — Outbreak Forecasting
50% outbreak probability by 2027; ~95% by 2035. The top three policy levers: methamphetamine effect on network density, incarceration rate, and SSP coverage. Policy must target these specific structural drivers to bend the curve.
Slide 13 — Return on Investment
A 10.5:1 five-year return on investment. 4.1 million additional successful transitions lead to ~100,000 HIV infections prevented annually, saving an estimated $40 billion in lifetime treatment costs. Bridge period navigation is one of the highest-value interventions in the PrEP implementation landscape.
Slide 14 — Validated. Scalable. Open.
Four pillars of implementation readiness. Validated at scale (21.2M, ±0.018 pp). Equity-focused (greatest benefits for PWID +265% and Adolescents +147%). High impact (~100K infections/year prevented). Implementation ready — open-source code available for immediate adaptation.
Slide 15 — Next Steps
Seeking prospective pilot sites for Phase 1 clinical validation. 50–100 patients, 3–6 months. The computational validation is complete — the next step is real-world clinical confirmation of the algorithm's impact on bridge period outcomes.

01 ‚Äî The Problem

A Guideline Without a Derivation

Post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP) can prevent HIV infection after exposure ‚Äî but only if started within a narrow window. The clinical guideline says 72 hours. Where does that number come from?

The uncomfortable truth

There is no formal mathematical derivation of the 72-hour threshold. The biological reality isn't a binary cutoff ‚Äî it's a continuous probability curve shaped by stochastic viral dynamics, reservoir seeding kinetics, and an irreversibility threshold where prevention becomes impossible. 02 ‚Äî The Prevention Theorem

Finite Windows Are a Mathematical Certainty

We prove that under irreversible infection establishment, all prevention windows are mathematically finite. The efficacy curve is continuous and nonlinear ‚Äî every hour matters, but some hours matter far more than others.

04 ‚Äî The Shadow Zone

When Treatment Looks Like It's Working ‚Äî But Isn't

Perhaps the most clinically important finding: the framework identifies a period where PEP suppresses measurable viremia while the latent reservoir has already been seeded.

Why this matters clinically

A patient in the shadow zone will show undetectable viral load on PEP. Their clinician sees success. But the integrated proviral DNA in resting CD4+ T cells is already there ‚Äî invisible to standard monitoring. When PEP ends after 28 days, the reservoir reactivates. The infection was inevitable from the moment the reservoir was seeded, regardless of how perfectly the patient adhered to treatment.

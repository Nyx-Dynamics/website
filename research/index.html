Bridging the Gap: The PrEP Cascade Paradigm Shift for Long-Acting Injectable HIV Prevention
Adrian (AC) Charles Demidont, DO — Nyx Dynamics, LLC
Long-acting injectable PrEP (LAI-PrEP) offers superior efficacy over oral PrEP, yet 47.1% of patients prescribed LAI-PrEP never receive their first injection. This study reconceptualizes the HIV prevention cascade for the LAI-PrEP era, defining the "bridge period" as a discrete structural vulnerability — the 2–8 week window between prescription and first injection — and proposing the Eliminate, Compress, Navigate framework to systematically close this gap. The paradigm shift: for LAI-PrEP, the primary barrier is pre-initiation structural failure, not post-initiation adherence.

Computational Validation of a Clinical Decision Support Algorithm for LAI-PrEP Bridge Period Navigation at UNAIDS PrEP Target Scale
Adrian Charles Demidont, DO — Nyx Dynamics, LLC
A clinical decision support tool (CDST) synthesizing evidence from 21 interventions was validated across 21.2 million synthetic patients — the UNAIDS 2025 global PrEP target — achieving policy-grade statistical precision (±0.018 pp). At scale, the tool predicts a baseline bridge period success rate of 23.96%, improving to 43.50% with evidence-based interventions: an 81.6% relative improvement representing 4.1 million additional successful transitions and an estimated 100,000 HIV infections prevented annually. Populations with the lowest baseline success rates — PWID (+265%) and adolescents (+147%) — showed the greatest relative benefit, demonstrating that systematic implementation narrows rather than widens health equity gaps.

Research Framework
Eliminate. Compress. Navigate.
⚡

Eliminate

Oral-to-injectable same-day switching for the 3.8 million patients already on oral PrEP. Existing negative test results and established clinical relationships remove the bridge period entirely — the single highest-impact intervention identified (+35% improvement).

⏱

Compress

Accelerated diagnostics using dual HIV testing (Antigen/Antibody + RNA) to reduce the mandatory window period from 45 days to 10–14 days, shortening the bridge period duration and the structural vulnerability window for patients who cannot eliminate it.

🧭

Navigate

Dedicated patient navigation, transportation assistance, telehealth integration, and harm reduction co-location for patients facing multiple co-occurring structural barriers. Patients with 3+ barriers face less than 15% probability of successful bridge period completion without targeted support.
